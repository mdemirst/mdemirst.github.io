I am a 2nd year master's student and a research assistant in Electrical Engineering Department at Bogazici University, Turkey. My research focuses on Robotics and Robot Vision. Specifically, I am working on "Vision Based Place Detection and Recognition". Previously, I worked in Baykar Aerospace Company, in which I worked as a part of the software team who built the first and most advanced Unmanned Aircraft in Turkey and currently being used by Turkish Armed Forces. I am also the co-founder of the first hackerspace, Base-Istanbul, in Turkey. I completed my undergraduate at Bogazici University. My long standing passion for Robotics has been started in my undergraduate years. I am a member of Intelligent Systems Laboratory since 2008 and currently doing my master in the same laboratory under the supervision of Prof. H. Isil Bozma. In the summer of 2010, I was a research intern in Biorobotics Laboratory at Carnegie Mellon University. In the following year, I was an exchange student in Tokyo, Japan and had another internship in Takanishi Humanoids Laboratory at Waseda University, Japan.

Upon completion of my M.S. degree, I would like to specialize either in Robot Vision or Autonomous Driving Systems. I am a permanent resident and authorized to work in the U.S.
